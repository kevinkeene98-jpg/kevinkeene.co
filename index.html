About Me

Cities and Transit

Originally from Chicago and moved to NYC in 2023, I've spent much of my life experiencing dense urban centers and vibrant public spaces. My close friends and family will tell you I can talk for hours about cities and my curiosity for them is endless.

Transit and high-speed rail are topics I'm borderline radical about, and I consider myself a social activist in this space. How we move through our cities and country lays the ground floor for quality of life and economic opportunities. The U.S. is far behind in this space and there's no reason we should settle for less.

Building and Growing Products

My career has been focused on developing products, and a lot of my interest in urbanism has shaped my thinking in my professional career. Cities have designs and systems in place that make them work, and I believe there's a lot we can learn from that and apply to building products.

The way users navigate through plans, how they experience different contexts, and the feedback loops that bring them back are foundations of great products and cities.

Much of my free time is spent building and launching products using AI coding agents. You can learn more about my work in the projects and experience sections.

Football and Volleyball

I grew up playing, watching, and loving most all sports. Football and volleyball stuck with me most. I played both in high school, and went on to play football in college.

Volleyball is where I'm most active currently, and I play rec league in New York.

I'm also a die-hard Chicago Bears fan and I watch just about every snap of every game. You can find me most Sundays at Triona's in Greenwich Village, one of the few Chicago Bears bars in New York City.
