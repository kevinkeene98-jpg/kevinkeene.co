About me

Cities and transit

Originally from Chicago and moved to NYC in 2023, I've spent much of my life experiencing dense urban centers and vibrant public spaces. My close friends and family will tell you I can talk for hours about cities and my curiosity for them is endless.

Transit and high-speed rail are topics I'm borderline radical about, and I consider myself a social activist in this space. How we move through our cities and country lays the ground floor for quality of life and economic opportunities. The U.S. is far behind in this space and there's no reason we should settle for less.

Building and growing products

My career has been focused on developing products, and a lot of my interest in urbanism has shaped my thinking in my professional career. Cities have designs and systems in place that make them work, and I believe there's a lot we can learn from that and apply to building products.

The way users navigate through plans, how they experience different contexts, and the feedback loops that bring them back are foundations of great products and cities.

Much of my free time is spent building and launching products using AI coding agents. You can learn more about my work in the projects and experience sections.

Football and volleyball

I grew up playing, watching, and loving most all sports. Football and volleyball stuck with me most. I played both in high school, and went on to play football in college.

Volleyball is where I'm most active currently, and I play rec league in New York.

I'm also a die-hard Chicago Bears fan and I watch just about every snap of every game. You can find me most Sundays at Triona's in Greenwich Village, one of the few Chicago Bears bars in New York City.

Projects

All my projects are built using AI coding agents. I'm not a coder by practice, but I do consider myself a technical and systems-minded product builder.

01

AI Axis

Why I built it: My team's AI costs were skyrocketing as we were building AI systems and agents. We had increasing hesitation to launch AI products because of the poor visibility into where costs were coming from and how to manage them. I built AI Axis to help monitor AI costs and identify opportunities to lower them.

How I built it: The system retrieves daily usage data from AI APIs (spend, tokens, requests) broken down by model and provider (OpenAI or Anthropic). I made a public version of this tool, with plans to launch on Product Hunt.

02

SEO Product Managers

Why I built it: My career started in SEO, and I made the transition into product. A number of my former colleagues noticed this transition and asked about how they can also get into product. I found it would be most helpful to create a job site that provides relevant job postings and AI-assisted resume and cover letter recommendations.

How I built it: The site fetches job postings from Google Jobs using SERP API. The resume builder reads the job postings, parses an uploaded resume, and provides actionable feedback through a chat interface.

SERP API AI resume builder Google Jobs
03

Corporate Slop Bowl

Why I built it: For fun! I enjoy the internet lore of corporate slop bowls. Don't get me wrong, I love Chipotle and the rest, but there's something to be said about how fast casual chains have gone too far into the corporate realm.

How I built it: The site is a personality quiz that matches your answers with a corporate slop bowl archetype (Chipotle, Cava, Sweet Green, etc.). It uses pre-prompted AI models to generate a unique response for every user.
