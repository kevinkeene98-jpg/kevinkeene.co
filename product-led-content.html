AI search & discovery

Product-led content

Why content should be treated as product infrastructure in the age of AI

For the past 20+ years, SEO strategy has started with the same question: What are people searching for?

That era is ending.

Search is shifting from navigational to conversational. No longer are people simply inputting a keyword, seeing a list of links, and landing on a website. The answers they are getting are now surfaced directly within conversational interfaces, synthesized from endless context and sources across the web.

That means targeting keywords no longer works. You don't become the default answer just because you rank for a keyword. That doesn't account for the infinite nuance that drives the conversations people are having with AI.

For example, consider a brand that ranks #1 for "best crm software". AI does use web search to inform certain responses, so it's reasonable to expect they will surface in most conversations about CRMs. But have you ever asked AI for "the best" of something and defaulted to the first option? Or was your conversation shaped by the very specific needs you had at the moment?

Someone searching for good CRM software has non-negotiable needs around pricing, team size, features, compatibility, use cases, and more. These are the things that shape their conversation with AI and ultimately will determine their buying decision.

When our options were listed in ten blue links on a search results page, maybe we had to compromise with what we found. But in a world where people have direct access to every available option, the question isn't only whether you'll be found — it's whether you'll be chosen.

AI will match people to the product that best fits their exact needs, and you'll only be chosen if you've addressed those needs. Your customers shape your product, and your product shapes your content.

That's product-led content.

Content isn't just a marketing asset — it's product infrastructure

Your content needs to be treated as an extension of the product. The features, how it's used, proof points, and value all need to be discoverable in the product as much as it needs to be surfaced on the web.

Content is your product on display to the world, much like your interface is the display for your users. To put this into practice, content builders need to understand your customers as much as the product builders.

This approach has been adopted by tech companies through the growing trend of "building in public". Building in public is the practice of openly sharing the process of creating a product, company, or project as it happens, including the wins, failures, decisions, and lessons learned. This has been a proven way of getting early and more quality product feedback while also cultivating a community of trust and interest in the product. Major AI companies like OpenAI and Anthropic have popularized this approach by openly publishing decisions made with the product and company, constantly reminding online audiences of their mission, what they're building, and why.

Building in public is an example of product-led content, and demonstrates how content can fuel the product feedback loop of discovery, adoption, and iteration.

Let your customers do the talking

If anyone knows your product, it's your customers. In most cases, they know it better than you. They're the ones using it and relying on it in their daily lives.

Listen to your customers first before you try prompt tracking and prompt research. Those are far-fetched ways at understanding your customers. It's impossible to truly understand your customers' needs unless you hear it from them directly.

Here's how to do that:

Build a culture around knowledge sharing with your customers. Give them a platform to speak their mind, whether that be what they love about the product, what can be improved, questions they have, or even just explaining how they use it.

This can take shape in public places like community sites, social media, case studies, or blog posts. Building in public is also a great way to get started giving your customers a platform to speak. People love to be seen and heard and amplifying your customers' voices will only help you.

This is how you make sure nuanced needs are met and publicized in a way that's discoverable.

Product-led content focuses on what you can control

You control your product and what you say about it.

Reddit threads, community forums, and socials can influence conversations but they don't shape the narrative of your brand. The narrative starts with your mission, is felt through the product, and is spread through your content.

You can't control what others say about your brand but you can control the story you tell that shapes the world. The rest will take care of itself.

Closing thoughts

Special thanks to Eli Schwartz, the trailblazer who popularized the term Product-led SEO. His work is on the forefront of product-first thinking when it comes to search and discovery, and Product-led Content largely builds on his vision.

SEO has long been understood strictly under the lens of being a marketing function. This restrictive framing limits the ability of search and discovery to become a channel for pipeline and conversion rather than just vanity traffic metrics.

The purpose of Product-led Content is to give a framework for content in the evolving search landscape. Many in the SEO space advocate for tactics like chunking, schema markup, forum messaging, etc. as being on the forefront of AI search strategy. These tactics may work in one-off cases, but they don't create systems that scale nor do they focus on outcomes for customers.

Search engines and LLMs don't care if you're implementing tactics — they care whether you're creating value for your customers. Product-led content is how you create value for your customers through content.
